# Pepla Brand Guidelines
*For independent creatives. An intuitive platform designed to meet you where you are — and grow with you.*
 
**Prepared by:** MacDoCo / Hope MacDonald
**Date:** March 2026
 
---
 
## Logo Guidelines
 
- Keep logo placement minimal and uncluttered
- Use neutral backgrounds whenever possible
- Logo appears in three formats:
  - **Horizontal lockup** — botanical icon + "PEPLA" wordmark in wide-spaced caps
  - **Stacked lockup** — botanical icon above "PEPLA" wordmark
  - **App icon** — botanical illustration on rounded-rectangle dark background with "Pepla" label beneath
 
The botanical motif is a hand-illustrated branch with rounded leaves, evoking organic growth and natural simplicity.

## Typography
 
### Primary Font — Dreaming Outloud (All Caps)
- Use for headings, logo wordmark, labels, and navigation
- All-caps only
- Humanist, slightly hand-crafted feel
- Pairs warmth with structure
 
### Secondary Font — Times New Roman
- Use for body copy, descriptions, captions, and longer-form text
- Italic variant used for editorial and expressive headers
- Serif elegance grounds the more playful primary font

## Visual Style & Imagery
 
- **Texture:** Film-textured, grainy, high-definition photography
- **Subject matter:** Desert landscapes, botanical close-ups, natural textures (rock, sand, water, fruit)
- **Tone:** Natural, balanced, consistent — supports clarity and flow across platforms
- **Color grading:** Desaturated, moody, dusty — leaning monochromatic with occasional deep reds
- **Feel:** Still, editorial, quiet confidence
 
### Do:
- Use imagery that feels grounded and organic
- Lean into grain and texture
- Keep compositions balanced and uncluttered
 
### Don't:
- Use overly polished or corporate stock photography
- Use bright, saturated, or high-contrast imagery
- Clutter layouts with competing visual elements
 
---
 
## UI & Layout References
 
*Inspired by Co-Star's interface.*
 
- Clean, content-forward layouts with generous whitespace
- Dark-mode and light-mode variants supported
- Card-based UI with subtle borders and minimal shadows
- Typography-led design — text does the heavy lifting
- Button style: understated, text-forward, with simple borders or underlines
- Navigation: sparse, editorial
 
---
 
## Brand Voice
 
- Direct and confident, but warm
- Speaks to independent creatives as equals
- Avoids jargon and corporate language
- Feels like a trusted tool, not a flashy product
